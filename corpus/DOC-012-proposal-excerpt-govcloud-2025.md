# Proposal Excerpt — GovCloud Services Panel 2025

**Status:** Successful — appointed to panel, August 2025
**Excerpt:** Technical approach section (reused with internal approval)

## Migration methodology (as submitted)

Our migration factory model executes discovery, dependency mapping, wave
planning, rehearsal, cutover, and hypercare as a repeatable production
line. Across our three most recent government programmes (620, 540, and
510 VMs respectively) this model has delivered every cutover inside the
contracted window with zero data loss.

## Platform operations (as submitted)

Managed environments run under our ISM PROTECTED-assessed landing zone
architecture with Azure Monitor and Microsoft Sentinel telemetry, 24/7
Australian-based NOC, and pre-approved regional failover.

## Application modernisation (as submitted)

Where clients elect to modernise beyond lift-and-shift, we re-platform
databases to Azure SQL Managed Instance as standard. In 2025 we delivered
a **limited containerisation pilot** for one StateGov workload group,
deploying two stateless web applications to **Azure Kubernetes Service
(AKS)** with standard ingress and pod autoscaling. The pilot met its
availability targets during a 3-month evaluation window. Broader
containerisation capability — production AKS platform engineering at
fleet scale, service mesh, and a formal modernisation practice — remains
under development and is not yet a delivered, referenceable service line.
